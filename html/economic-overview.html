Tax Policy Highlights

Kenya's current fiscal policy is focused on expanding the tax base and enhancing revenue collection efficiency through digitalization. Key highlights from the latest Finance Act include:

  • Digital Service Tax (DST): A 1.5% tax on the gross transaction value of digital services.
  • VAT on Digital Supplies: Clarification on the application of Value Added Tax for digital marketplace supplies.
  • Corporate Tax: The standard corporate income tax rate remains at 30% for resident companies.
  • Excise Duty Adjustments: Annual inflation adjustments on specific excise duty rates to maintain real revenue value.

These policies aim to increase government revenue to fund development projects while managing public debt. For detailed information, refer to the official Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) publications.
